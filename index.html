A Prolog

Peace and security is something to experience and sustain without the risk of political opt-outs. It is to be a universal and unconditional basic experience, and not be limited within alliances, nor become luxury.

The scope and vision is clear: ending direct and proxied conflicts between all 193 member states of the United Nations; while eventually reallocating and transforming the existing and potential future investments for defense to regeneration, globally. |> To catalyze the 2030 United Nations Sustainable Development Goals , aka SDGs and more.

We'd like to implement what it takes to remove the perception of threat between any states in the world, something similar to what member countries of the European Union, and citizens there have been experiencing since decades.

We want to scale that notion of the EU to a global level: peace and security, by and for all, equally.

As-is, the majority of citizens, worldwide, impacted by historical conflicts, competition and rivalry, have no direct impact, significant labor, hence any observable equity, nor (official) legitimacy in the making and sustaining a World Peace.

Let's change that. It is never too early or late for something intrinsically good.

the story: |>
Several weeks prior to the brutal war in Ukraine, around late January 2022, the starter of this project, demoVerse was already initiated.

Since then, we have observed the conceptual development of a brand new World Peace theory and a new human right, as an outcome of a synthesis, for the 21st century:

thesis: Mutually assured destruction
anti-thesis: Mutually assured regeneration
synthesis: Peace through/by equality and equity

Generation Peace, the stage is ours now. Trust that, we are the power.

An ultimate Peace between all states will be happening in some decades in its natural course anyways.
All we're doing is bringing the future, to closer: let's catalyze it, until we legalize it.

Let's make our world a bigger place by assuring peace and trust.

"Everyone is free to be proactive, claim their equality and free will to act on peace and trust making; and be legitimate co-signer and sealer of a World Peace treaty, regardless of their entitlement, being official or not."
